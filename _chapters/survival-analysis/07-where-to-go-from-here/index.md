Advanced techniques such as Cox proportional hazards modeling (Cox, 1972) address this by allowing you to:
1.  **Adjust for confounders**: Account for patient-related factors (covariates) like age or disease stage that might distort the results.  
2.  **Analyze multiple factors**: Assess the impact of several variables on survival simultaneously (multivariate analysis).
3.  **Estimate effect sizes**: Go beyond just testing for differences (like the log-rank test) to estimating the actual strength of a factor's impact on survival.
